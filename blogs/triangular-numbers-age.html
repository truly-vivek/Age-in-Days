What Are Triangular Numbers?

Triangular numbers are the sum of the first “n” natural numbers. They are called triangular because they can be arranged in the shape of an equilateral triangle. The first few triangular numbers are:

  • 1 (1st triangular number)
  • 3 (2nd triangular number)
  • 6 (3rd triangular number)
  • 10 (4th triangular number)
  • 15 (5th triangular number)

These numbers are calculated by adding up the integers sequentially. For example, the 3rd triangular number is 1 + 2 + 3 = 6.
